Quarterly Dispute Volume

Disputes filed surged from 149 in Q1 2023 to 14,986 in Q4 2024, representing a 100.6× increase over eight quarters. The sharp acceleration beginning in Q4 2023 suggests a strategic shift toward systematic out-of-network billing.

Market Share Evolution

HRA's share of total IDR market peaked at 5.0% in Q4 2023 before declining to 1.6% by Q4 2024, despite continued absolute volume growth. This indicates the overall IDR market is expanding faster than HRA's already-rapid growth trajectory.

Arbitration Win Rates

Win rates remained exceptionally high throughout the study period, ranging from 82.6% to 99.6%. The weighted average win rate of 93.9% suggests HRA's documentation and subspecialty expertise arguments are highly persuasive to arbitrators.

Payment Outcomes

When prevailing in arbitration, HRA receives payment at approximately 105% of the Qualified Payment Amount—a consistent 5% premium across all quarters. This modest premium suggests HRA's billed rates align closely with what arbitrators consider fair market value.

Key Findings

Volume Acceleration

The 100.6× increase in dispute volume from Q1 2023 to Q4 2024 represents one of the most dramatic growth patterns observed in the dataset. This suggests a fundamental strategic shift toward out-of-network billing, potentially driven by contract terminations with major insurers or changes in the Houston Methodist system's payer relationships.

Arbitration Success

HRA's 93.9% overall win rate—prevailing in approximately 46,000 of 48,680 disputes—substantially exceeds typical arbitration outcomes. This performance suggests that HRA's documentation, subspecialty expertise credentials, and association with Houston Methodist's premier academic medical center provide strong justification for rates above the Qualified Payment Amount.

Modest Premium Structure

The consistent 5% premium above QPA stands in marked contrast to out-of-network providers seeking 200-300% premiums. This moderation suggests either that HRA's billed rates are close to what arbitrators consider fair market value for subspecialist radiology services, or that the competitive dynamics of the Houston market constrain pricing power despite HRA's exclusive provider status.

Market Context

HRA's declining market share (from 5.0% to 1.6%) despite absolute volume growth indicates that the overall IDR market experienced even more rapid expansion in 2024. This suggests an industry-wide trend toward out-of-network strategies and systematic IDR utilization, raising questions about the sustainability of the No Surprises Act's arbitration mechanism.

Policy Implications

The data demonstrates that physician ownership does not inherently prevent high-volume IDR utilization. HRA, an independent practice since 1943, exhibits billing patterns comparable to private-equity-backed competitors. This suggests that financial incentives and market position—rather than ownership structure—are the primary drivers of out-of-network strategy, pointing toward the need for payment methodology reform rather than ownership-focused regulation.
